# EU Regulatory Compliance

## European Accessibility Act (Directive 2019/882)

EU Lens aligns with the European Accessibility Act requirements for:

- **Digital Services**: Making websites and mobile applications accessible
- **E-commerce**: Accessible transaction processes
- **Digital Content**: Accessible information and documentation

### Compliance Measures
- WCAG 2.2 AA compliance as minimum standard
- Screen reader compatibility
- Keyboard navigation
- Adjustable presentation settings
- Alternative text for non-text content
- Captioning for audio content

## EU AI Act

EU Lens follows a risk-based approach to AI governance:

### Risk Classification
- **Misinformation Detection**: Limited risk — user-facing transparency and human oversight
- **Content Simplification**: Minimal risk — assistive technology
- **Trust Scoring**: Transparency requirement — explainable scores

### Compliance Measures
- Transparent AI decision-making
- Explainable trust scores
- Human oversight options
- Bias monitoring and mitigation
- User education on AI capabilities and limitations

## Digital Services Act (DSA)

EU Lens supports DSA objectives through:

- Content moderation transparency
- User empowerment tools
- Media literacy support
- Transparent recommendation systems
- Accessibility obligations

## GDPR Compliance

- **Data Minimization**: Only essential data collected
- **Purpose Limitation**: Data used only for core functionality
- **User Rights**: Full data access, correction, deletion, and portability
- **Privacy by Design**: Privacy considerations built into architecture
- **Data Security**: Encryption, access controls, regular audits

## Ethical AI Framework

Based on the EU High-Level Expert Group's Guidelines for Trustworthy AI:

1. **Human Agency and Oversight**: Users maintain control, with AI as assistive tool
2. **Technical Robustness and Safety**: Fallback mechanisms, error handling
3. **Privacy and Data Governance**: GDPR-compliant data handling
4. **Transparency**: Explainable AI decisions, clear labeling
5. **Diversity, Non-discrimination, and Fairness**: Accessibility-first design, bias monitoring
6. **Societal and Environmental Well-being**: Public interest focus
7. **Accountability**: Clear responsibility, audit trails
